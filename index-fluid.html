How it works

Pour. Filter. Bottle.

A multi-stage pipeline runs every week: scan wide, score hard, keep almost nothing. Only the clearest opportunities reach your inbox.

01

Wide intake

AI crawls forums, subreddits, app stores, and search data to surface recurring pain points with no good solution yet — hundreds of candidates, every single week.

02

Five-stage distillation

Each candidate is scored on SEO gap, competition density, monetization clarity, build feasibility, and audience reachability. Fail one stage and it dissolves.

03

The weekly pour

What passes arrives as a full brief: the pain point, why it's blue ocean, the monetization path, and exactly where your first 100 users already gather.

Why so complex and specific?

Because the obvious ideas are already gone. "Build an AI writing tool." "Make another todo app." The column dissolves these in seconds — oversaturated, margin-less, already built by a thousand people.

What survives is narrow, vertical, and requires domain knowledge. That's not a bug — that's the moat. If it were obvious and easy, someone would have done it.

DentalAuth AI
SaaS

Dental offices spend 30+ minutes writing a single Prior Authorization letter — manually, every claim. Formatting errors cause denials. No purpose-built AI tool understands CDT codes and insurer-specific narrative structure.

Why blue ocean
Generic AI tools don't know CDT codes or PA narrative structure. SERP for "dental prior auth letter generator" is empty. High-intent B2B pain with zero dedicated tool.
Monetization
Per-seat subscription $29–79/mo for dental offices. Clear ROI: one approval pays for months of the tool.
Cold start
DentalTown forums and dental billing Facebook groups. "Dental prior auth letter template [insurer]" — one page per insurer, zero competition.
ForDental office managers, billing staff
ClaimSnap
SaaS

Independent CAT adjusters convert field notes into carrier-ready structured loss reports by hand — each carrier has different formatting requirements. During CAT season this bottleneck kills throughput.

Why blue ocean
Enterprise tools (Xactimate) start at $200+/mo targeting staff adjusters, not independents. No lightweight AI report generator exists for CAT independents.
Monetization
Per-report credits during CAT season. Annual subscription for year-round adjusters. Carrier white-label for high-volume distribution.
Cold start
NAPIA community and CAT adjuster Facebook groups. Storm season timing makes organic distribution natural.
ForIndependent CAT adjusters
Deep build 3–6 months · defensible data advantage
DroneCompli
Subscription

Commercial UAV operators working across borders face different airspace rules, BVLOS authorizations, and operator licensing requirements in every country. Official sources are scattered, poorly translated, and rarely updated.

Why blue ocean
No neutral database covers global commercial UAV compliance in one place. The fragmentation is the moat — once aggregated, no one can replicate it quickly. Aviation lawyers charge $300+/hr for what this automates.
Monetization
Subscription for commercial operators + per-seat for fleet teams. Compliance consultancies pay for API access.
Cold start
r/drones and DJI commercial operator communities. "[Country] commercial drone BVLOS rules" — programmatic page per country, each uncontested.
ForCommercial drone operators, aviation lawyers
FontLegal
Freemium

Designers and AI trainers don't know if their fonts allow commercial embedding, broadcast use, or AI training rights. Font license PDFs are dense legal documents. The risk of a violation surfaces only after shipping.

Why blue ocean
No searchable matrix covers embedding/broadcast/AI training rights across major font libraries. AI training restrictions emerged in 2023 — newly urgent, unserved, legal teams now scrambling.
Monetization
Freemium with bulk-check premium; design agency team plans. AI company compliance licenses at enterprise pricing.
Cold start
r/graphic_design, Typewolf community, HuggingFace dataset builders. "[Font name] commercial license AI training" — every major font is an uncontested long-tail page.
ForDesign studios, app developers, AI engineers
PAClear
Subscription

Medical billing managers submit Prior Authorization requests without knowing historical approval rates per CPT code and insurer — wasting hours on submissions with a 90% denial probability.

Why blue ocean
No tool translates CPT × insurer denial rates into pre-submission intelligence. RCM software shows your historical outcomes — not market-wide approval odds before you submit.
Monetization
Subscription per billing department; RCM consulting firms pay for white-label. Reducing denial rate by 10% saves large practices $50K+/yr.
Cold start
Medical Billing & Coding Facebook groups, AAPC forums, r/medicalbilling. "[CPT code] prior auth denial rate [insurer]" — one uncontested page per CPT × insurer combination.
ForMedical billing managers, physicians
AuditScore
Subscription

DeFi protocols pay $50K–$500K for security audits — then get exploited anyway. Which auditors actually catch critical bugs? The data is public but scattered across postmortems and Discord threads.

Why blue ocean
DefiLlama tracks hacks but not auditor accountability. No database maps audit firm → missed exploits → dollar losses. Protocol teams make six-figure decisions based on Twitter rep, not track record.
Monetization
Subscription for protocol teams; institutional risk managers and DAO treasuries pay premium API rates. The stakes make $99/mo a trivial due-diligence expense.
Cold start
DeFi security Twitter/X, r/ethdev, Immunefi bug bounty ecosystem. Post an audit firm comparison thread — distribution is immediate in a community starved for this data.
ForDeFi protocol teams, institutional risk managers

Monetization

Cold start
EOR procurement Slack communities, LinkedIn HR/legal groups. "[Platform] contractor misclassification [country] 2024" — one page per platform × country, each uncontested.
